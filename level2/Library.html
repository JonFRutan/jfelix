Books

Crime and Punishment - Dostoevsky

This is a contender for the greatest book I've read. Dostoevsky masterfully displays feelings of turmoil, guilt, loss, all in a beautifully cold and somber setting.

Crime and Punishment is the story of how misery warps the soul of a man into a monster. More importantly though, is Dostoevsky's message of redemption and acceptance, that no person can be too far beyond

The writing can be a bit complex at times, and the Russian naming convention might be confusing at first. Working past these, you'll find a book that touches deep into the spirit and psyche of struggle.

I wholeheartedly recommend everyone read this.

A Farewell to Arms - Hemingway

This book is rather quick and simple to read. Almost deceptively simple though; as it does have some interesting themes veiled by Hemingway's unique dialogue and rather blunt writing.

I generally enjoyed most of the book, a wartime romance with vividly written scenery. I will mention that the book takes a rather unexpected turn at a certain point, that quite affects the entire story.

I would recommend this book to those who like a straightforward narrative, and can handle serious and dark themes.
